## 🌍 International Laws

### United States

#### Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA)
- **18 U.S.C. § 1030**
- Unauthorized access is a federal crime
- Penalties: Up to 20 years imprisonment
- Fines: Up to $250,000

#### Electronic Communications Privacy Act
- **18 U.S.C. § 2510-2522**
- Prohibits interception of communications
- Penalties: Up to 5 years imprisonment

#### State Laws
- Many states have additional computer crime laws
- California: California Penal Code § 502
- Texas: Texas Penal Code § 33.02
- New York: New York Penal Law § 156.00

### European Union

#### General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
- **Regulation (EU) 2016/679**
- Any data accessed must be protected
- Disclosure requires lawful basis
- Violations: Up to €20 million or 4% of global turnover

#### NIS Directive
- **Directive (EU) 2016/1148**
- Security requirements for essential services
- Incident reporting requirements
- National penalties apply

#### ePrivacy Directive
- **Directive 2002/58/EC**
- Communications confidentiality
- Consent requirements
- Cookie regulations

### United Kingdom

#### Computer Misuse Act 1990
- **c. 18**
- Unauthorized access: 2 years imprisonment
- Unauthorized access with intent: 5 years
- Unauthorized modification: 10 years

#### Data Protection Act 2018
- Implements GDPR in UK
- Information Commissioner's Office enforcement
- Fines up to £17.5 million

### Canada

#### Criminal Code of Canada
- **R.S.C. 1985, c. C-46**
- Section 342.1: Unauthorized use of computer system
- Punishment: Up to 10 years imprisonment
- Section 342.2: Possession of hacking tools

#### Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA)
- Data protection requirements
- Privacy breach notification
- Office of the Privacy Commissioner enforcement

### Australia

#### Cybercrime Act 2001
- **Cth**
- Unauthorized access: 2 years imprisonment
- Unauthorized modification: 10 years
- Creation of hacking tools: 3 years

#### Privacy Act 1988
- **Cth**
- Australian Privacy Principles
- Data breach notification
- Fines up to AUD 2.1 million

### Germany

#### Strafgesetzbuch (StGB) § 202a
- Data espionage: Up to 3 years
- Hacking: Up to 5 years
- Preparation for hacking: Up to 3 years

#### Bundesdatenschutzgesetz (BDSG)
- Federal Data Protection Act
- DSGVO (GDPR) implementation
- Heavy fines for violations

### China

#### Cybersecurity Law
- Data localization requirements
- Security reviews
- National security compliance

#### Personal Information Protection Law (PIPL)
- Effective November 2021
- Similar to GDPR
- Heavy penalties

### India

#### Information Technology Act 2000
- Section 43: Unauthorized access
- Section 66: Computer-related offenses
- Penalties: Up to 3 years imprisonment
- Section 70: Protected systems

#### Personal Data Protection Bill
- Expected to pass soon
- Similar to GDPR
- Heavy fines for violations

## 🔒 Data Handling

### If You Find Data:

1. **DO NOT**: Access or view sensitive data unnecessarily
2. **DO NOT**: Share or distribute data
3. **DO NOT**: Use data for personal gain
4. **DO**: Report findings to authorized parties
5. **DO**: Secure your findings properly
6. **DO**: Delete data when no longer needed

### Data Classification

| Type | Classification | Handling |
|------|----------------|----------|
| PII/Personal Data | HIGH | Report without storing |
| Credentials | CRITICAL | Hash or redact |
| Business Data | CONFIDENTIAL | Report securely |
| System Data | INTERNAL | Include in technical findings |
| Public Data | PUBLIC | May be included in reports |

### Data Retention Policy

| Data Type | Retention Period | Method |
|-----------|------------------|--------|
| Test credentials | 24 hours | Delete immediately after testing |
| Vulnerability findings | 30 days | Secure storage |
| Extracted data | 7 days | Secure storage |
| Logs | 90 days | Secure storage |
| Final reports | 1 year | Encrypted storage |

### Secure Storage Guidelines

```bash
# Encrypt data
gpg --symmetric --cipher-algo AES256 sensitive_data.zip

# Secure deletion
shred -v -n 5 sensitive_data.sql
rm -f sensitive_data.sql

# Secure transfer
scp -c aes256-ctr report.pdf user@secure-server:/path/
```
